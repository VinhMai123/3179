What countries are our top Univerisities located in?

Out of the top 2000 ranked univeristies around the world, 347 of them belong in America. The top 8 schools in America are known as Ivy League Schools and got there name after the 1954 after the formation of the athletics conference. The next country with the second most ranked univeristies in the world is China with over 277 univerisites. Despite this, many Chinese students prefer to study abroad due to univerisites in America and United Kingdom having more prestigous options.

The top 10 Univerisities in the World

This bar graph on the left shows the top 10 ranked Univerisities in the world. They are all very similar in score with Harvard Univerisity being the only University to have a perfect score of 100. Out of the 10, eight of them belong to America and two belong to the United Kingdom . Harvard Univerisity was established in 1636 and is known as the oldest institution of higher learning in the America and is one of the most prestigious and highly rated in the world (Wikipedia). Many famous people have attended Harvard Univerisity during their life. These include names such as Bill Gates (Founder of Microsoft), Michelle Obama (Former First Lady of the United States), and Mark Zuckerberg (Founder of Facebook).

National Rank vs. Global Rank of the top 20 Ranked Univerisities

The scatterplot on the right compares the national and gloabl ranking of the top 20 Universities in the world. Harvard Univerisity remains at the top both nationally and globally. Many of these Univerisities have pretty consistant rankings. However, there are some Universities that rank much higher nationally than globally. One example we can see here is from the Univerisity of Tokyo. In Japan, it is the best ranked University. However, when compared globally, it struggles to break the 10, ranking at 13th in the world. University of Cambridge is also number 1 in the UK. However, its ranking falls short of America's top three Ivy League Schools.
